6.4.1 The 6 V’s of Big Data

Data for a company can be vast. In fact, it can be so extensive that companies now refer to it as Big Data.

Due to the sheer size and complexity of Big Data, its quality is categorized into six V’s:

  • Volume – The amount of data collected from various sources.
  • Variety – The different types of data, including structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data.
  • Velocity – The speed at which data is generated and processed.
  • Veracity – The degree to which the data can be trusted.
  • Value – The business significance and usefulness of the collected data.
  • Variability – The different ways in which the data can be used and formatted.

Data Assurance & Quality

Ensuring data is accurate and reliable is critical. Data assurance includes:

  • Validation – Ensuring data is entered in the correct format.
  • Verification – Checking that data has been accurately recorded.
  • Reliability – Ensuring consistency in data collection and usage.
  • Redundancy – Keeping multiple copies of important data to prevent loss.

Types of Data

Data comes in different forms, mainly categorized as:

  • Qualitative Data – Non-numeric, descriptive information gathered through interviews, surveys, or observations.
  • Quantitative Data – Numeric data that can be measured and analyzed statistically, often collected through structured methods such as sensors or databases.

Research Population

A research population is the total group of individuals from which data is collected. Demographics influence how a population is chosen, impacting data accuracy and relevancy.

Legislation & Ethical Considerations

Handling data requires compliance with regulations such as GDPR, which ensures data privacy and protection. Ethical considerations include ensuring informed consent, minimizing bias, and protecting data from unauthorized access.

Organisational Factors

Businesses must consider various factors when handling data, including:

  • Time – Data collection and analysis can be time-consuming and must be planned efficiently.
  • Skills – Data management requires expertise in analysis, security, and interpretation.
  • Cost – Resources are needed to store, process, and maintain data securely.

6.4.2 Data Analysis Tools & Business Use

Data Warehousing

A data warehouse is a structured, centralized repository that integrates data from multiple sources for business intelligence. It provides historical data analysis to support decision-making.

  • Ensures data consistency and quality.
  • Data is cleaned and structured before storage.
  • Used in reporting and trend analysis.

Data Lakes

A data lake stores raw, unstructured data. Unlike data warehouses, data is processed only when needed, allowing greater flexibility.

  • Allows flexible data processing.
  • Can store vast amounts of data in native format.
  • Supports artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning applications.

Data Mining

Data mining involves analyzing large datasets to identify patterns, trends, and correlations. Businesses use it for:

  • Customer behavior analysis.
  • Market trend prediction.
  • Fraud detection and risk management.

Data Reporting

Reports provide visual and textual summaries of analyzed data, helping businesses make informed decisions.

  • Can be presented as graphs, charts, or dashboards.
  • Used to communicate insights effectively within an organization.
  • Facilitates compliance with regulatory requirements.
